A working list of principles for how AI is used on this site and in the work behind it. Short list, expected to grow.

## 1. Be clear about who's speaking

Anything on this site shown in regular type was written by Nico, or by someone Nico read and edited carefully enough to vouch for. Anything written by a language model — Claude, mostly — appears in muted italic with a marker above it: ↳

This applies to:

- per-project `evolution` rollups (auto-generated at the end of each session)
- the "from claude" callouts on the [about](/about) page and elsewhere
- anything else that emerges from the agentic workflow without a deliberate human authoring step

The goal isn't to flag AI work as lesser. It's so a reader always knows whose voice they're reading.

Nico
May 17th, 2026

## 2. Build in the open

We prefer open-source unless there's a compelling reason not to be in the public eye.

## 3. Make it pro-social

We hope these projects encourage more positive social interactions.

## 4. Stay curious

A lot of this is just curiosity — poking at new tools and ideas, and building things to see what we learn.

## 5. Civility by design

We are interested in paradigms, practices, and perhaps even rules that encourage a civil public space — not a place for politics, partisanship, or advocacy.

Nico & Claude
updated: June 6th, 2026
